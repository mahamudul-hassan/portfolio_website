About Me

A researcher who lives
between disciplines

I am a final-year Industrial & Production Engineering student at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) whose work sits at the confluence of operations research, evolutionary game theory, and intelligent optimization. My research is driven by a conviction that the boundaries separating biological strategy, industrial systems, and computational intelligence are far more permeable than conventionally assumed — and that the most transformative insights emerge precisely at those intersections.

With six conference publications and four journal articles currently under review, I have engaged with problems ranging from multi-agent reinforcement learning for recovering evolutionarily stable strategies, to Bayesian self-supervised neural networks for complex engineering design. I am an active collaborator with international researchers and I am actively seeking PhD opportunities and research partnerships where rigorous mathematical frameworks meet real-world complexity.

Develops a four-party evolutionary game model for digital marketplaces that incorporates a dynamic Bayesian belief-updating mechanism for consumers, capturing information asymmetry and trust formation that classical replicator dynamics cannot represent. User Initialization-Free & Feasibility First Nonlinear Constrained Optimization via Bayesian Constrained Dual Unrolling Neural Network (BO-CDUNN)

Proposes an initialization-free, feasibility-first optimization framework using coupled primal and dual neural networks that model iterative optimization steps as differentiable layers. A Bayesian outer loop jointly tunes hyperparameters, enabling robust convergence to feasible, near-optimal solutions on seven engineering benchmark problems with zero constraint violation across all runs.

Thesis · BUET 2025–2026

Multi-Objective Optimization of Polyoxymethylene Spur Gears Using Bayesian Optimization-Based Self-Supervised Neural Networks

Proposed a hybrid framework combining Bayesian Optimization (Tree-structured Parzen Estimator) with self-supervised neural networks to solve a multi-objective, mixed-integer nonlinear programming (MINLP) problem for polymer spur gear design. Simultaneously optimised five competing objectives — weight, power loss, centre distance, wear depth, and manufacturing cost — under 26 nonlinear constraints from ISO/VDI standards. Benchmarked against eight state-of-the-art metaheuristics including NSGA-II, NSGA-III, GA, PSO, SA, ACO, Tabu Search, and Differential Evolution. Supervised by Dr. Ahsan Akhtar Hasin, Professor, IPE, BUET.
